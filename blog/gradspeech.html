"THANK YOU! GOODBYE!"

Jun 27, 2020
Facebook
Twitter
LinkedIn

My graduation speech as the Student's Council President, in front of all students, teachers and parents:

Dear school staff and friends, and parents. Greetings to all on this glorious day. I take this opportunity to thank the principal and the school staff for today's wonderful organization. Moreover, I congratulate the directorate at the beginning for supporting some organizations, many of them are many for the first time in our school, thus bringing are new and interactive.

Now I see how lucky I've been, starting from the first grade where I had the opportunity to meet the best teacher in the world, then with the most loyal and best friends, and with a homeroom teacher who sacrifices to push you further. I'm starting with the one who gave me the first steps... Irena Gegaj Prenaj, a name that, for me, but undoubtedly also for my friends, is of great importance. Firstly a parent, then a teacher, and a fantastic friend who stood by us at every step. Friends... a lot to say. One a joker, the other stubborn, one a math passionate, the other a science enthusiast, and the other a technology lover. One given after sports, the other after poetry drama... We all have reached that level to be free to talk to each other about things that we wouldn't talk to anyone else. Undoubtedly, this connection has kept us in an inseparable friendship, and I have no doubt that it will remain so for years.

It is no coincidence that he has been recognized as the 'Best Homeroom Teacher' because he really is. Honored and highly respected, Mr. Fatmir Krasniqi, the first time I saw you were somewhere in the fourth or fifth grade when you came to the class and assessed our potential. In the following year, you would take us as our Homeroom Teacher.

The way you teach makes you enjoy the Albanian language as a subject, even if one didn't like it at all. And when it comes to motivation and stimulation, who would have thought that we would be able to visit the beauty of Boga, the coast of Shengjin, the grandeur of the Shkodra Castle of Kruja, and more, as early as in the seventh grade. And on this occasion, if you were to ask me to sum it all up in one word, that word would be: "THANK YOU!"

Thank you to all teachers who, with their professionalism, did their best to teach us their subject, and not only. We have received many diverse tips and instructions from them. Grammar, regular and irregular verbs in English, equations, alkenes, and alkanes, biotope, Newton and Coulomb, World War I and World War II, Earth and Relief, Children's rights Volleyball of Basketball, Beethoven and the Symphony, Binary Numbers and Visual Perspectives were just some of the lessons' titles, which I'm very grateful for your teachers' contribution.

All this praise, all deserved, I want to return once again to ourselves, the students. How many quarrels, covers, and similar. How many childish things we have gone through, for which we are sorry if we've broken the rules, all those we've gone through a good friendship. But, you know what? Congratulations to you all, really. We have gone through many times that have intentionally or unintentionally become barriers to functionality. However, in the end, it is the success of our tireless work over the years. We never gave up, even in the most challenging moments. And since the end has come, wishing you health in the first place, further success, and luck in life, I wanted to conclude this speech with a tip: Do not wait for the opportunity to come before your feet. Even a saying says: 'If the opportunity doesn't knock, it is because there is no door, so build one.'

We are at a level where we have feelings of emotion, an extremely high and sometimes uncontrollable one. Still, the most remarkable human power is to control itself even in the right moments. In the end, I feel obliged to ask for three applauses; let the first applause go to us, the students... (applause); the second to the school teachers and the school staff (applause); and last but not least, to parents themselves, as unparalleled supporters... Thank you for the time. I hope we are always like that. As a student of the "Elena Gjika" school, as the president of its Youth, I wish you further success for all! Goodbye!
